In the “boom” of the foreclosure market, REOs have become a popular investment tool for both institutional and private investors. Known as “real estate owned”, these properties have been returned to the bank after attempts were made to sell the property through foreclosure auctions. Since banks are in the business of lending money, not owning property, properties are offered in bulk at steep discounts. The “over-stock” of REOs today, is directly connected to the failure of many banks and lenders. Too much inventory and not enough operating capital means banks are offering their REO packages at historically low rates. Without an REO Specialist investors do not have an established inner-bank resource to truly maximize on REO opportunities and usually face mark-ups, or sales from third party liquidators who have diluted the margin of profitability.

PIP-West Offers Investors a Direct Link To Its Inner-Bank Resources
Typically, in a strong market a good REO Specialist can acquire viable property for about 80 cents on the dollar. In TODAY’S market, good, rent/move-in ready property can be acquired for as little as 50 cents on the dollar. Most REO liquidators buy these properties in bulk, mark them up to about 70, or 80 cents on the dollar and re-sell them individually. Others will convey that they are selling them for 50 cents on the dollar, against the loan value. Here’s How Investing in REOs with PIP-West Works
  1. Before becoming a client of PIP-West, we will consult with you and discuss your investment objectives. This includes determining the area you want to purchase property, whether you want PIP-West to just assist in the acquisition, or if you want us to handle the property management, liquidation, maintenance, etc. Once we determine the best course of action for you, you will become a client of PIP-West
  2. You will provide PIP-West with a Letter of Intent (“LOI”) outlining the terms of your REO request (value, property type, location, etc.), as well as Soft Proof of Funds. PIP-West will validate your proof of funds and qualify you as a buyer.
  3. You, PIP-West, the Bank Resource and the Escrow/Closing Attorney will execute a Non-Disclose/Non-Compete agreement (“NDNC”). Once all forms/contracts have been properly executed, PIP-West, on your behalf, will submit your request to the bank resource for a listing of REOs based on the terms you provided in your LOI. (Click here to download a sample NDNC/LOI form)
  4. The bank will research and provide PIP-West/you a listing of properties to choose from (usually about 50). You will have two weeks from the date we/you receive the listing to perform any due diligence on the property. PIP-West handles all due diligence on your behalf, to include physically viewing the property, checking comps, current fair market values, taxes, etc. We report those findings to you and you direct us as to which, if any, properties you wish to purchase. (Click here to download a sample PIP-West Property ProForma)
    Note: You are under no financial obligation with the bank to purchase any property provided in the listing and can request additional listings.
  5. Once you have selected the properties for purchase from your listing, the remaining portion of the transaction works in the same method as it did when you purchased your home. Purchase Agreements/Contracts are signed, the closing attorney is provided with the information and your funds are placed into escrow. PIP-West will attend the closing on your behalf and provide you with the Warranty Title to the properties following the closing.
